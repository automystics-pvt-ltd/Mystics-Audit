# Business Scope & Product Direction

The application is designed primarily for **IT services, software development, SaaS products, project management, and software consulting businesses**. It is **not** intended for businesses that primarily sell physical products, hardware, devices, or retail inventory.

The system should be optimized for a software company's day-to-day operations, including:

* Project Management
* Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC)
* SaaS Subscription Management
* IT Service Delivery
* Software Sales
* Software Licensing
* Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMC)
* Support & Helpdesk
* Customer Relationship Management (CRM)
* Lead & Opportunity Management
* Proposal and Quotation Management
* Contract Management
* Resource & Employee Management
* Time Tracking & Timesheets
* Billing & Invoicing
* Expense Management
* Purchase of IT Software, SaaS subscriptions, cloud services, domains, SSL certificates, hosting, development tools, and other IT-related services
* Vendor Management for software and technology service providers
* Financial management aligned with service-based businesses

The Purchasing module should be tailored for **software and service procurement**, such as:

* Software Licenses
* SaaS Subscriptions
* Cloud Services
* Development Tools
* API Services
* Third-Party Integrations
* Security Software
* IT Consulting Services
* Hosting & Infrastructure Services
* Digital Assets

While the application should support inventory concepts where necessary (for example, software licenses or digital assets), the primary business model is **service-oriented**, not product-oriented. The workflows, terminology, dashboards, reports, and business processes should reflect the operational needs of an IT company rather than a manufacturing, trading, or retail business.

The platform should be built as a **multi-tenant SaaS application**, allowing multiple organizations to use the system independently while maintaining complete data isolation, subscription management, role-based access control, and tenant-specific configurations.

The architecture should also be **future-ready and extensible**. Although the initial focus is on IT services and software businesses, the design should allow additional business verticals, product categories, and industry-specific workflows to be introduced later without requiring major architectural changes. New modules and business processes should be configurable and integrate seamlessly with the existing platform.
